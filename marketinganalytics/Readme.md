📊 Hartnow Technologies Marketing Simulation (Harvard Business School)
📌 Overview
This project analyzes strategic decision-making in a Harvard Business School marketing simulation, where we managed Hartnow Technologies and optimized growth over a 10-period timeline. The goal was to maximize market valuation, revenue, and customer lifetime value (CLV) through data-driven marketing and investment strategies.

🎯 Objective
To identify the most effective combination of marketing investments, customer segmentation, and operational strategies to drive long-term growth and maximize market valuation.

📈 Results
Achieved ~$242.8M market valuation in the top-performing strategy
Significant growth observed across simulation periods, especially after mid-phase strategic adjustments
Improved customer retention, contract value, and revenue stability

🧠 Strategy & Approach
1. Experimentation Across Strategies

Tested multiple approaches including:

CRM report timing
Sector-specific investments
Loyalty vs referral programs
Budget allocation strategies
Full spend vs reserved budget approach

2. What Worked Best
Early CRM Investment (Periods 1–3)
Improved customer satisfaction and retention
Increased customer lifetime value (CLV)
Customer Retention Focus
Loyalty (Period 4) and Referral Programs (Period 6)
Reduced churn and increased repeat purchases
Shift to High-Value Customers
Focused on premium segments
Increased contract value and long-term revenue
Branding & Customer Service Investment
Strengthened positioning
Reduced acquisition costs and improved pricing power

3. Segmentation Strategy
Automotive
Highest growth in revenue and contracts
Became primary driver of market value
Defense
Strong retention and long-term contracts
Increased stability and CLV
Aerospace
Slower growth but consistent revenue
Helped diversify risk

⚠️ What Didn’t Work
Holding excess budget → slowed growth
Over-distributing spending across sectors
Delayed premium product launch
Early heavy investment before stabilizing churn/NPS

🔄 Key Improvements Identified
Invest in CLV/CCAV earlier
Launch premium offerings sooner
Optimize spending across high-ROI sectors
Improve churn tracking and reduce lost revenue
