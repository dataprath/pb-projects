🚁 Amazon Prime Air – Drone Delivery Network Optimization

📌 Overview
This project designs a data-driven supply chain network for Amazon Prime Air’s 30-minute drone delivery program across Massachusetts. It focuses on identifying optimal hub locations using analytical modeling, demand segmentation, and operational constraints.

🎯 Objective
To determine the most efficient and scalable hub placement strategy that enables fast, reliable drone deliveries while considering regulatory, geographic, and demand-based constraints.

🧠 Approach
📍 1. Network Optimization
Applied the Center of Gravity (COG) model to identify optimal hub locations
Minimized distance between demand clusters and fulfillment hubs

📊 2. Demand & Segmentation Analysis
Analyzed population density and income distribution
Identified high-demand regions for rapid delivery adoption
Segmented markets into priority zones

⚖️ 3. Constraints Considered
FAA regulations impacting drone flight zones
Drone capability (MK30) – range, payload, and operational limits
Landing feasibility (residential vs commercial areas)
SKU eligibility for drone delivery

🗺️ 4. Rollout Strategy
Phase 1: Western & Central Massachusetts (low-risk, lower density)
Phase 2: Expand to suburban regions
Phase 3: Scale into high-demand Eastern Massachusetts

🛠️ Tools & Technologies
Excel / Python – Data analysis & modeling
GIS / Mapping tools – Location analysis
Optimization techniques (COG model)
Data visualization for decision support

📈 Key Outcomes
Identified optimal hub locations to minimize delivery time
Built a scalable rollout strategy aligned with operational feasibility
Balanced demand coverage with regulatory and technical constraints

💡 Key Insights
Western/Central MA offers a controlled environment for initial deployment
Eastern MA presents highest demand but requires stricter regulatory navigation
Drone constraints significantly impact serviceable SKUs and delivery radius

🚀 Future Improvements
Incorporate real-time traffic & weather constraints
Use advanced optimization (linear programming / ML models)
Expand to multi-state network design
